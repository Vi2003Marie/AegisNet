Understanding Threats
Brute Force Attempt
A device is repeatedly trying to connect to a service port like SSH (22) or RDP (3389). This could be an attacker trying to guess a password. Investigate or block the source IP.
Port Scan
A device is scanning multiple ports rapidly. This is typically the first step attackers take to discover what services are running before launching an attack.
Traffic Flood / DDoS
A device is sending an unusually high number of packets in a short time. This could be a Denial of Service attack attempting to overwhelm the network or a server.
What To Do
1. Identify the source IP from the alerts table.
2. Check if it is a known device in Connected Devices.
3. If unknown or suspicious — block it immediately.
4. Export the logs as CSV for further investigation.
